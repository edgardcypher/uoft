Assignment 3: Detailed Design for an Information System

Due Date: 12:00 midday Friday December 6, 2002 (to BA 5234)
This assignment counts for 20% of the final grade

Prepare a detailed design for the information system you have been working on for assignments 1 and 2. The detailed design should include selection of hardware, networking and software for the new system, design of a global architecture, a detailed design for the classes that are part of the system, a relational database schema and suitable I/O procedures and interfaces.

This assignment is to be carried out by teams of three. Teams of two will be allowed when no third member is available. Each team will submit one report.

I. Doing the Assignment

This assignment has eight steps. They are:

1. Review your requirements specification from assignment 2, and revise it on the basis of the comments you received from the marker.

2. Define a global architectural design for the system, consisting of computer networks (existing or new), hardware (existing or new) and software platform (operating system and any other commercial software you will be using for your system).

3. Select hardware, software and networking to be purchased for the new system. Specify clearly the criteria and alternatives you are using for your decision.

4. Propose a software architecture for the new system and justify your choice.

5. Prepare a detailed design of the classes that are part of your system, using detailed class diagrams. Define signatures for all attributes and operations. Include interaction and state diagrams to show detailed object interactions.

6. Design the database schema for the database component of your system. Take into account workloads in proposing a relational database schema. Use both class diagrams and ER diagrams to describe the contents of your database before you generate a relational schema.

7. Design prototype user interfaces for the use cases identified in your analysis model. Also, define any other external interfaces (e.g. with other software systems, specialized devices, etc)

8. Write a report describing  the complete system design. The report should include as an appendix the requirements specification on which your design was based and a careful account of how all requirements (functional and non-functional) were addressed in the design.

II. What to Hand In

Hand in your report to the professor by midday on the due date. Reports not handed in by 12.01pm will be treated as late.

Your report should not exceeding twenty-five (25) pages (not counting references, appendices, figures or tables). Assume that the assignment you hand in will be read by the programmers responsible for implementing the system. This means that the technical details need to be presented clearly, and your report should provide enough rationale to enable the programmers to understand the design decisions you have made. 

Written Presentation Requirements

Drawings must be clear and legible. Be sure to put on a cover page indicating the names of all team members, title of work, course, date and tutor's name. Assignments will be judged on the basis of visual appearance, grammatical correctness and quality of writing, as well as their contents.

Please make sure that the text of your assignment is well-structured, using paragraphs, full sentences, and other features of a well-written presentation. The report must not consist of itemized lists of points. Text font size should be either 10 or 12 points.

III. The Marking Scheme

The marking scheme for this assignment will include the following factors:

Global Architecture (20%): Includes a description of the global architecture of your system, as well as the hardware, networking and software platform on which it will run; also includes the global software architecture of your system;

Detailed Program Design (20%): Includes UML diagrams which describe in detail all system classes and their interactions;

Detailed Database Design (20%): Includes the relational schema of your system's database and the process by which it was derived from class diagrams;

Detailed Interface Design (20%): Covers the design of interaction classes and user interfaces;

Supporting Documentation (10%): Supporting documentation regarding the selection of a computing platform, team meetings, meetings with the customer organization, etc.

Presentation (10%): The overall quality of the presentation, as in previous assignments.
 
Note: The top three assignments will be posted on the course website, and will get for this a 5% bonus on this assignment.
