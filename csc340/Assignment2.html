Assignment 2: Preparing a Requirements Specification
 

Due Date: 12 midday, Friday, November 8 (to BA5234)
This assignment counts  for 15% of the final grade
 
Prepare a requirements specification for an information system project of your own choice, which relates to an existing organization. This project is meant to give you exposure to basic concepts discussed in the course, and also to encourage you to "look around" for the type of work you may be doing in a few years as computer science professional.

The project is to be carried out by teams of three. Each team will submit one report.

I.   Doing the Assignment

This assignment has seven steps.  They are:

1. Choose a problem for which the development of a new (or replacement) information system is a suitable solution. You can use the problem and proposed solution that you identified in your feasibility report. Alternatively, you can use a problem identified by another team in their feasibility report (in which case that team gets a 2% bonus on assignment 1);
2. Describe the problem and the alternative you have selected in detail (you may want to go beyond what was done in the feasibility study, or correct flaws the marker pointed out);
3. Identify the requirements, including all functional and non-functional requirements for the new system, the data that the system will need to manage, interfaces to other systems, and interfaces for different classes of users;
4. Prepare use case, class, sequence, and state diagrams, and a glossary to describe in more detail the functional and data requirements for the new system;
5. Discuss the requirements you have generated with the contact person(s) in the customer organization, and get feedback. Don't forget to describe any meetings, discussions and feedback in an appendix of your report.
6. Write a report that describes the problem you chose, the requirements you identified, the models you generated, and the discussions you had with the client.
7. Document your teamwork and complete a team report (see attached form).

II.   What to Hand In

Hand in your report to the professor by midday on the due date. Reports not handed in by 12:01pm will be treated as late.

You should hand in a report of requirements analysis, not exceeding twenty-five (25) pages (not counting references, appendices, figures or tables). Assume that the assignment you hand in will be read by managers and software designers. This means that the document part of the assignment should be readable by non-technical readers, while the UML diagrams and more technical details should be presented in appendices. As with the feasibility study, make every effort to be clear and concise about your recommendations in your presentation.

Written Presentation Requirements

Drawings must be clear and legible. Be sure to put on a cover page indicating the names of all team members, title of work, course, date and tutor's name. Assignments will be judged on the basis of visual appearance, grammatical correctness and quality of writing, as well as their contents.

Please make sure that the text of your assignment is well-structured, using paragraphs, full sentences, and other features of a well-written presentation. The report must not consist of itemized lists of points. Text font size should be either 10 or 12 points.

III.  Marking Scheme

Marks for this assignment will depend on the following factors:

Requirements specification (30%): Completeness, consistency and clarity;

UML diagrams (40%): Completeness and correctness of your diagrams;

Supporting evidence (10%): Appendices which describe meetings and other information on the preparation of the assignment; supporting documentation for your UML diagrams;

Presentation (20%): The style of your presentation, including language, grammar, clarity of the presentation etc.
 10% - Language
 10% - Style and clarity

Note: The top three assignments will be posted on the course website, and will get for this a 5% bonus on this assignment.
